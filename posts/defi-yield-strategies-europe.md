## Choosing the Right Strategy

Your ideal strategy depends on your risk tolerance:

**Low risk (1-3% APY)** — Deposit directly into Aave V3. You're using the most battle-tested lending protocol with the deepest liquidity. This is the "set and forget" option that minimizes smart contract risk.

**Medium risk (3-6% APY)** — Use Morpho vaults or aggregators like YO Finance. You get higher yields through professional optimization, but you're adding another smart contract layer on top of the base lending protocol.

**Higher risk (variable APY)** — Provide liquidity on decentralized exchanges like Curve or Uniswap. Returns depend on trading volume and can fluctuate significantly. This requires more active management.

## Managing Your Risks

### Smart Contract Risk

Not all protocols are equal. Stick to audited protocols with long track records. Aave has been running for years and holds billions in deposits. A newer protocol might offer higher yields, but hasn't been through as many stress tests. Start with smaller amounts on newer protocols.

### Depeg Risk

EUR stablecoins can temporarily lose their 1:1 Euro peg during extreme market stress. Larger, regulated issuers like Circle have stronger peg mechanisms — EURC has maintained tight peg stability through multiple market events.

### Yield Sustainability

If a yield looks too good to be true, investigate why. Very high APYs sometimes come from temporary incentives that won't last. Check TVL trends — growing TVL with stable yields is a healthy sign. Shrinking TVL with rising yields can indicate risk.

### Diversification

Don't put everything in one protocol. Spreading deposits across 2-3 protocols on different chains reduces your exposure to any single smart contract failure or chain issue.
