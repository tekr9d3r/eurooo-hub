## Why Earn Yield on EURC?

EURC is Circle's MiCA-compliant euro stablecoin, fully backed by euros held in regulated EEA financial institutions. It trades at parity with the euro and can be redeemed 1:1 at any time. Unlike a euro sitting in a bank account earning near-zero interest, EURC can be put to work in DeFi protocols that offer meaningfully higher yields — often well above what European banks offer on savings.

This guide covers every major protocol where you can earn yield on EURC in 2026, with current rates, risks, and instructions for getting started.

## Where to Earn EURC Yield

### 1. Aave V3 — Simple Lending (Base)
**Approximate APY: ~2.17%**

Aave is the largest decentralised lending protocol in DeFi. On Base, Aave V3 supports EURC as a supply asset — you deposit EURC, and borrowers pay interest to use it. Your deposit earns the variable supply rate automatically.

- **Risk level:** Low. Aave is one of the most audited and battle-tested protocols in DeFi.
- **Impermanent loss:** None. You deposit EURC and withdraw EURC.
- **Liquidity:** Withdraw at any time.
- **Best for:** Conservative users who want predictable, low-risk yield.

### 2. Morpho — Curated Lending Vaults (Base)
**Approximate APY: Variable (optimised above Aave)**

Morpho sits on top of lending protocols like Aave and routes capital to maximise yield across curated markets. The **Moonwell Flagship EURC vault** on Morpho Blue (Base) is specifically designed for EURC:

- Curated by B.Protocol and Block Analitica
- One-token deposit — you supply EURC only, no pairing required
- Allocates to blue-chip collateral markets
- No impermanent loss risk

- **Risk level:** Low to medium. Morpho adds a layer of complexity over Aave but is well-audited.
- **Best for:** Users comfortable with DeFi who want better yields than Aave without liquidity pool exposure.

### 3. Merkl — Incentivised Yield Distribution
**Approximate APY: Up to 36.9%**

Merkl is a yield distribution protocol that aggregates incentives from multiple sources. The highest EURC yields currently available on-chain come through Merkl's incentive programs, which layer additional token rewards on top of base lending or liquidity yields.

- **Risk level:** Medium to high. The high APY reflects temporary incentive programs — rates are variable and can change significantly.
- **Important:** The 36.9% figure includes incentive token rewards that may not be in EURC. Monitor rates actively.
- **Best for:** Experienced DeFi users comfortable with variable, incentive-driven yield.

### 4. Curve Finance — EURC/USDC Liquidity Pool
**Approximate APY: ~10% (historical)**

Curve is the leading DEX for stablecoin-to-stablecoin trading. The EURC/USDC pool on Curve lets you provide liquidity and earn trading fees from users swapping between the two assets.

- **Risk level:** Low to medium. Stablecoin pools on Curve have minimal impermanent loss since both assets are pegged to fiat currencies (EUR and USD respectively).
- **Note:** There is some FX exposure — if EUR/USD moves, the relative value of your pool position changes.
- **Best for:** Users comfortable with liquidity provision who want exposure to FX trading fees.

### 5. Uniswap & Aerodrome — EURC/USDC Pools (Base)
**APY: Variable (trading fees)**

Uniswap V3 and Aerodrome (Base's leading DEX) both have EURC/USDC pools. You provide liquidity in a price range and earn a share of trading fees from swaps.

- Uniswap: 0.05% fee tier for the EURC/USDC pair
- Aerodrome: competitive fees plus potential AERO token incentives
- **Risk level:** Medium. Concentrated liquidity positions require active management to remain in range.
- **Best for:** Active liquidity managers and DeFi power users.

### 6. Nexo — Fixed-Term CeFi (Up to 11.25% APY)
**Approximate APY: Up to 11.25% (fixed-term)**

For users who prefer centralised custody, Nexo offers fixed-term EURC deposits with higher rates than flexible savings.

- **Risk level:** Medium (counterparty/custody risk with a CeFi platform).
- **Liquidity:** Locked for the term duration.
- **Best for:** Users who prefer a simple interface and are comfortable with CeFi custody.

## How to Get Started: Step by Step

1. **Acquire EURC** — Buy EURC on a supported exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance) or redeem euros directly via Circle. Make sure you have EURC on the correct chain (Base for most DeFi options).
2. **Set up a wallet** — Use MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or any WalletConnect-compatible wallet. Add the Base network if it's not already configured.
3. **Bridge if needed** — If your EURC is on Ethereum, use the Base Bridge or a third-party bridge to move it to Base.
4. **Choose a protocol** — For simplicity, start with Aave V3 on Base. For higher yields, explore Morpho or Merkl.
5. **Supply or deposit** — Connect your wallet to the protocol, approve the EURC token spend, and deposit your tokens.
6. **Earn automatically** — Interest accrues continuously. Your balance grows in real time on lending protocols.
7. **Withdraw anytime** — On Aave and Morpho, you can withdraw at any time (subject to utilisation rates). Fixed-term products like Nexo lock funds for the agreed period.

## Risks to Understand Before Depositing

- **Smart contract risk:** All DeFi protocols can contain bugs. Stick to well-audited protocols with long track records.
- **Variable rates:** APYs on lending protocols change based on supply and demand. The rate you enter at is not guaranteed.
- **Impermanent loss:** Liquidity pools (Curve, Uniswap, Aerodrome) expose you to IL, especially if EUR/USD fluctuates.
- **Incentive volatility:** High APYs on Merkl depend on ongoing incentive programs. These can end or change without notice.
- **Liquidation:** If you borrow against your EURC on Aave, your collateral can be liquidated if its value falls below the required threshold.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I earn yield on EURC without impermanent loss risk?**
Yes. Aave and Morpho are lending protocols where you supply EURC and earn interest without providing it as one side of a trading pair. There is no impermanent loss.

EURC itself is MiCA-compliant and fully backed by euros in regulated EEA institutions. The risk is in the DeFi protocols you use, not the stablecoin. Use well-audited protocols and understand the risks of each.

**Which chain should I use for EURC DeFi?**
Base offers the broadest EURC DeFi ecosystem with low transaction fees. It is the recommended starting point for most users in 2026.

**Do I need to do anything to earn yield?**
Once you deposit into a lending protocol like Aave or a vault like Morpho, yield accrues automatically. No claiming required — your balance increases in real time.
