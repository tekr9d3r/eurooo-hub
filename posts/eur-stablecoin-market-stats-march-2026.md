## EUR Stablecoin Market Overview — March 2026

As of March 1, 2026, the total circulating supply of EUR-denominated stablecoins has reached **€702.30 million**, marking a **3.79% increase over the past 30 days**. There are currently **19 EUR stablecoins** tracked across **26 blockchains**.

The EUR stablecoin market continues to grow steadily, crossing the €700M milestone for the first time. MiCA-regulated issuers dominate the rankings, and institutional engagement from traditional banks is becoming a defining characteristic of the European on-chain economy. This monthly report covers the latest market cap data, market share distribution, and chain breakdown for every major Euro stablecoin.

## Top 10 EUR Stablecoins by Market Cap

Here is the full ranking of the ten largest Euro stablecoins by circulating supply as of March 2026.

**1. EURC (Circle) — €415.6M (64.3% market share)**
EURC remains the clear dominant force in the EUR stablecoin market. Issued by Circle and fully MiCA-compliant, EURC is live on Ethereum, Base, Solana, Avalanche, and six additional chains. Its circulating supply grew slightly from February, maintaining its position as the go-to EUR stablecoin for both retail and institutional DeFi users.

**2. EURCV / EUR CoinVertible (SG Forge) — €62.2M (9.6%)**
EUR CoinVertible from SG Forge — Societe Generale's digital asset subsidiary — has grown to €62.2M, up from €58.2M in February. EURCV is available on Ethereum and Solana, and remains the largest EUR stablecoin issued by a traditional financial institution.

**3. AEUR / Anchored Coins AEUR — €51.4M (8.0%)**
AEUR holds third place with €51.4M across Ethereum and BSC, though its lead over EURI has narrowed to just €0.4M this month.

**4. EURI / Eurite — €51.0M (7.9%)**
EURI (Eurite) has climbed to €51.0M on Ethereum and BSC — nearly level with AEUR. The battle for third place in the EUR stablecoin market is tightening.

**5. EURE / Monerium EUR emoney — €25.8M (4.0%)**
Monerium's EURE continues to be one of the most widely deployed EUR stablecoins by chain count, live on Gnosis, Ethereum, Arbitrum, Polygon, and five additional networks. It holds a 4.0% market share at €25.8M.

**6. EURR / StablR Euro — €12.4M (1.9%)**
EURR, issued by StablR, is available on Ethereum and Solana, holding a 1.9% market share.

**7. EUROP / Schuman EUROP — €8.2M (1.3%)**
Schuman's EUROP is available on Ethereum, Polygon, Avalanche, and Plasma, with a 1.3% market share.

**8. EURS / Stasis Euro — €8.0M (1.2%)**
One of the oldest EUR stablecoins in existence, EURS is available on Ethereum, Polygon, Stellar, Algorand, and three additional chains. Despite its history, it holds just 1.2% market share as newer MiCA-compliant issuers have outgrown it.

**9. EURA (formerly agEUR) — €3.7M (0.6%)**
EURA is available on Ethereum, Arbitrum, Polygon, Fantom, and six additional chains.

**10. CEUR / Celo Euro — €3.4M (0.5%)**
CEUR is available on Celo, Solana, Polygon, and Ethereum, with a 0.5% market share.

## Blockchain Distribution of EUR Stablecoins

EUR stablecoins are deployed across 26 blockchains. The three largest chains by EUR stablecoin supply are:

- **Ethereum** — 70% of total EUR stablecoin supply
- **Solana** — 12%
- **Base** — 11%

Together, Ethereum, Solana, and Base account for **93% of all EUR stablecoin supply**. Ethereum's share has grown from 68% in February to 70% in March, reflecting continued institutional preference for mainnet. Other supported chains include Gnosis, Avalanche, Polygon, and BSC.
